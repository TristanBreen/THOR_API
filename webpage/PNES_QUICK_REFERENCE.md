## Common Questions

**Q: Does a HIGH score mean I have PNES?**  
A: No. It means your pattern has features common in PNES. Only a doctor can diagnose.

**Q: Can my score change?**  
A: Yes. As you track more data and patterns emerge, your score will update.

**Q: What if my score is LOW but I think it's PNES?**  
A: Low scores suggest epilepsy patterns, but PNES can occur with any seizure-like symptoms. Discuss with your doctor.

**Q: Should I stop my seizure medication?**  
A: No. Never stop without medical guidance, regardless of your PNES score.

**Q: How is this different from an EEG?**  
A: This analyzes patterns in your seizure data. EEG directly measures brain activity. Both can help with diagnosis.
